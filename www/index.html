FAQ
1. App

Q: The app isn't working, what should I do?

A: If you have not yet started doing the intervention, try uninstalling and reinstalling the app. Make sure to delete not only the app, but also the app’s cache memory through your system preferences. Please do not uninstall the app if you have already started the intervention. If the problem persists, or you have already completed part of the intervention, please contact us at consumption.jrp@gmail.com.

Q: My notifications aren't working, what should I do?

A: You should receive notifications from the app every day for one week (or until you finish the week-long intervention) at 17:00/5pm in your local time. If you have not received a notification from the app, check that the app’s notification setting is set to ‘on’ in your phone’s app settings: for Android and Apple devices.

If the notifications are set to ‘on’, and you still do not receive notifications, try turning your phone’s mobile data off and then on again. If this doesn’t work, and you have not yet started doing the intervention, try uninstalling and reinstalling the app.

Make sure to delete not only the app, but also the app’s cache memory through your system preferences. Please do not uninstall the app if you have already started the intervention. If the problem persists, please contact us at consumption.jrp@gmail.com.

Q: How do I turn the notifications off?

A: If you are still enrolled in the study, please do not turn off your notifications. These notifications are important in reminding you when to log-in to the app and complete the intervention.

Notifications will automatically stop when you complete the intervention. If, for any reason, you would still like to turn the notifications off, you may do so through this linked guidance for Android and Apple devices.

Q: How do I remove the app from my phone?

A: You can remove the app just like any regular mobile app. Simply uninstall the app either through your system preferences or on your homescreen, with a long press on the app icon.

Q: I uninstalled the app, but then changed my mind and reinstalled it. Can I continue the study where I left off?

A: If you wish to continue the study after deleting and reinstalling the app, please contact us at consumption.jrp@gmail.com.

2. Data

Q: When will I be able to see my progress report?

A: You will receive a progress report after the 7 day intervention and upon completion of the study. The study will be considered complete after both the 7 day intervention and the 1 month follow-up.

Q: How can I see my data?

A: If you wish to access your data please contact us at consumption.jrp@gmail.com.

Q: Will others be able to access my data?

A: No, we use a reliable and secure web-service to store and encrypt the data you submit through the questionnaires. Only the research-team will be able to access the data. After the study, all personal information will be destroyed.

3. The study

Q: I forgot my login information and/or am having difficulties logging in to the survey, what should I do?

A: If you have forgotten your login information or are having difficulties logging in, please contact us at consumption.jrp@gmail.com so that we can assist you.

Q: I skipped a day, what should I do?

A: If you accidentally skipped a day of the intervention, please carry on from where you left off.

For example, if you were unable to complete the “Day 5 questionnaires” on your 5th day of participation in the study, please complete them on your 6th day of participation. Carry on in the same way by completing the “Day 6 questionnaires” on the 7th day and the “Day 7 questionnaires” on your 8th day of participation.

Who are we?

This intervention is developed by a group of international Psychology scholars aiming to help consumers reduce excessive consumption.

The mobile application used in this intervention study was developed by Yuval Herziger.

For more information, please visit our website.

Background and purpose

This is a request for you to participate in a week-long study aimed at reducing your non-essential purchasing behavior. The goal of the study is to gain a better understanding of ways to reduce excessive consumption.

What does the study entail?

In order to participate in this intervention study, you have been asked to download a free application to your smartphone. All participation will take place through this app.

The intervention will last for 7 days. The first and the last day will require the most participation from you, namely 10-15 minutes. Each of the 5 remaining days will only demand 5 minutes of your time.

On the first day, you will be asked to answer some questions about your spending habits and some questions related to your personality. We will also ask you about some demographical information, such as age, gender, income, and education.

On each of the following days, you will be asked to answer a couple of questions about your mood. You might also be presented with some additional material. Finally, you will be asked to recall the number and costs of some of your recent purchases, if any.

On the last day of the study, we will again ask you about your spending habits and personality. Lastly, you will be asked to reflect on your own consumption behavior. Throughout the study, your smartphone application will ping you once a day, kindly reminding you to continue your participation.

After one month following your participation in the intervention, we will send you an email containing a link to a short online questionnaire.

Lottery prize & additional benefits

Upon completion of participation, you will be eligible to win a lottery prize of 100 euro. In order to be eligible to take part in the lottery, you must have completed the full one-week intervention and your one-month follow-up assessment. One winner will be randomly selected from all complete survey responses. The research team will email the winner to coordinate the prize transfer. You will also receive information about your personal progress relating to your spending behavior upon study completion.

Legal disclaimer

Requirements of participation

In order for you to participate in this study, you need to fulfill a few requirements. You must agree with the following criteria:

  1. I am over 18 years of age
  2. I have a desire to reduce my non-essential spending (e.g., clothes)
  3. I have not previously followed the social media trend promoting “Minimalism” (i.e., simplistic living)
  4. I have no clinical diagnosis related to impulse control or addictions (such as compulsive buying disorder and hoarding)
